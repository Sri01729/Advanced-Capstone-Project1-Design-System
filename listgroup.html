Design System

Overview

In design systems, lists are commonly used to present information in a structured way, whether it's a simple list of items or a more complex hierarchy of information. Lists can be useful in a variety of contexts, such as navigation menus, product features, and content sections, as they can help to break up large blocks of text and make it easier for users to scan and find the information they need.

When to use

When using lists in a design system, it's important to ensure that they are consistent in terms of formatting, spacing, and typography, and that they follow a clear hierarchy to make the content more scannable. It's also important to consider the accessibility of lists, such as ensuring that they can be navigated using keyboard controls.

When not to use

However, lists may not always be the best option for presenting information, particularly if the content is complex or requires more detailed explanations. In such cases, alternative formats such as tables or interactive visualizations may be more appropriate.
