Design System

Overview

Tooltips are used to provide additional information or clarification about a specific element or feature in an application. They can contain text, images, or other media and are often used to explain the function of a button, link, or other UI element.

When to use

Tooltips are useful when:

  • The information being presented is short and does not require a full screen or dedicated space.
  • The information is supplementary or additional to the main content.
  • The user needs quick access to information about a particular element or feature.

When not to use

Tooltips may not be appropriate when:

  • The information is critical or necessary to the user's understanding of the interface.
  • The information is too complex or lengthy to be presented in a small tooltip.
  • The user's device or environment does not support hover or click events.


Overall, tooltips can be a useful design tool in the right context, but they should be used judiciously and with consideration for the user's needs and environment.
