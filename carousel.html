Design System

Overview

A carousel is a user interface component that displays a series of images or content items in a rotating manner. The carousel typically displays a subset of content at a time, with users being able to navigate between items using arrows, dots, or swipe gestures.

When to use

Carousels are often used to showcase products, highlight features or services, or display portfolios. They can be effective in capturing users' attention and showcasing a variety of content in a small space. Additionally, carousels can provide an interactive and engaging experience for users.

When not to use

However, there are some cases when carousels may not be the best choice. For example, if the content in the carousel is critical or important, users may miss it if they do not interact with the carousel. Additionally, if the carousel is not designed well, it can be confusing or difficult to use, leading to frustration for users. It's also worth noting that carousels can impact website performance and increase load times, especially if the images or content items are large or numerous.


Overall, when considering whether to use a carousel, it's important to weigh the benefits of showcasing multiple pieces of content against the potential drawbacks of decreased visibility or usability. If you decide to use a carousel, be sure to design it with user experience in mind and optimize it for performance.
