Design System

Overview

A space scale is an important aspect of any design system. It establishes a consistent and harmonious system of spacing that can be used throughout a product, website, or application. Our design system uses the below spacing guidelines:

we mainly use five different types of spacing. They are

Space-1(Extra small)

This is the smallest amount of space in the scale, and is typically used for small elements like icons or labels. An example of an extra small space could be 4px.

Space-2(Small)

This is slightly larger than extra small and can be used for things like small buttons or tight text spacing. An example of a small space could be 8px.

Space-3(Medium)

This is the most common space size and is used for most elements in a design. An example of a medium space could be 16px.

Space-4(Large)

This is a relatively large space size and is used for things like section breaks or large buttons. An example of a large space could be 32px.

Space-5(Extra large)

This is the largest amount of space in the scale and is used for significant spacing between major design elements. An example of an extra large space could be 64px.
