Design System

Overview

Toggles are used in design systems to enable users to swiftly flip between two states. They are frequently used to choose between two alternatives or to turn features or settings on or off.

When to use

Toogle's are useful:

  • When you must make it simple and quick for consumers to convert between two states.
  • When the option is binary, such as true/false or on/off.
  • When the toggle's status is immediately obvious, like when on and off are represented by distinct colours.

When not to use

Toogle's may not be appropriate in this situations

  • Use checkboxes or radio buttons if there are more than two alternatives available.
  • Use a drop-down menu or a select component when the option needs extra explanation.
  • The user's device or environment does not support hover or click events.


When utilizing toggles, it's crucial to take accessibility into account and make sure that everyone can understand their function and condition, including users of assistive technology.
