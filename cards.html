Design System

Overview

A card is typically a container with a defined width and height, which holds a variety of content such as text, images, and action buttons. They are often used to group related information in a clear and concise manner.

When to use

Cards are a good choice when you want to:

  • Display a collection of related items, such as products, blog posts, or news articles
  • Present information in a visually engaging way
  • Provide users with quick access to actions or additional information

When not to use

Cards may not be the best choice when:

  • The information is not related or does not need to be grouped
  • Space is limited, and you need to display a large amount of information in a small area
  • The information is not suited for a visual presentation, such as large tables or data sets.


Overall, cards can be a useful component in a design system, but it's important to consider the context and content being displayed before choosing to use them.
